Refactor the analysis output into a structured "decision brief" layout. The current analysis feels unstructured, repetitive, and too long. It should be concise, scannable, and focused on the most important insights.

Key design principle:
Users should immediately see the key tensions and where experts agree or disagree before reading the rest of the analysis.

Reorganize the analysis page into this new structure:

1. Decision summary
- One short sentence describing the decision being evaluated.
Example:
“Should Aql focus its initial niche on B2B SaaS founders?”

2. Core issues / key tensions
- Show the 2–3 most important trade-offs driving the decision.
- Each tension should be one short line.
Example:
• Regulatory risk(Legal)vs market opportunity (Business Development)
• Technical debt vs product reliability
• Customer concentration vs growth potential
- List which departmets disagree here 

3. Expert alignment
Split into two small sections:

What experts broadly agree on
- 2–3 concise bullet points summarizing shared views and who agrees

Where experts disagree
- Highlight the key trade-offs between departments.
- Show disagreements as short bullets like:
  • Legal vs Financial – regulatory exposure vs revenue opportunity
  • Technical vs Business Development – product reliability vs rapid market entry
- show this in a creative way 

4. Recommended path
- Show this prominently after the tensions and alignment.
- Include:
  • path name
  • 1–2 sentence explanation for why it is recommended.

5. Paths forward
Show the available options as clear cards or sections.

Each path should include:
- title
- 1 short description
- favored by (personas)

Example:
Move Now  
Immediate focus on B2B SaaS founders leveraging existing insights.  
Favored by: Business Dev

Pilot Program  
Run a limited pilot with selected founders to validate demand.  
Favored by: Legal, Financial

Delay and Research  
Conduct deeper research before committing resources.  
Favored by: Technical, Tax

6. Next steps
- Show only 3–5 concrete next steps.
- Each step should be a short actionable item.
- show this in a timeline kind of diagram

Example:
• Develop compliance framework  
• Build targeted founder outreach strategy  
• Establish strategic partnerships

7. Persona score snapshot
- Display all persona scores in a compact visual format.
Example:
Business Dev – 80  
Financial – 62  
Legal – 64  
Tax – 65  
Technical – 70

8. Detailed persona reasoning
- Move this into collapsible sections or an accordion.
- Hidden by default.
- Only visible if the user wants deeper analysis.

Cleanup rules:
- Remove duplicated information between sections.
- Avoid large text blocks.
- Avoid repeating persona analysis multiple times.
- Keep wording concise and executive-level.
- Make the page easy to scan quickly.

Tone:
- clear
- analytical
- executive
- founder-focused
- no generic AI language.

Goal:
The page should feel like a premium strategic decision brief where a founder can quickly understand:
• what the real issues are  
• where experts agree and disagree  
• what the recommended action is.
